# The 1953 Iranian Coup and Kermit Roosevelt Jr.'s August 19, 1953 "Operation Ajax" CIA Execution — How a Grandson of Theodore Roosevelt's $60,000 Bribery Network, Tehran Bazaari Mobilization, and Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi's Waffling Loyalty Overthrew Prime Minister Mohammad Mosaddegh's Nationalized Oil Government, Created the 25-Year SAVAK Secret Police Dictatorship, and Established the Covert Regime-Change Template for 21st-Century CIA Bay of Pigs Planning, 1954 Guatemala PBSUCCESS, and U.S.-Saudi Intelligence Partnership in Yemen

## Summary

The research team discovered that the 1953 Iranian coup was not a spontaneous popular uprising but a meticulously orchestrated CIA-SIS operation led by Kermit Roosevelt Jr., whose $60,000 bribery network purchased the appearance of popular will through compromised clerics, bazaari merchants, and rent-a-mobs. The operation's success created a transferable template for covert regime change that directly informed the disastrous Bay of Pigs invasion, the 1954 Guatemala PBSUCCESS operation, and enduring U.S.-Saudi intelligence partnerships—while simultaneously bequeathing Iran a 25-year SAVAK dictatorship that would ultimately produce the 1979 revolution and decades of anti-American hostility rooted in the original intervention.

## Key Findings

### The Operational Architecture of Operation Ajax

- **Kermit Roosevelt Jr.'s central role**: The grandson of Theodore Roosevelt served as the CIA's chief architect of the coup operation, personally directing activities from Tehran. This represented a striking departure from his grandfather's "big stick" diplomacy toward clandestine manipulation of foreign political systems. [Kongzi: 1, 3, 6]

- **The $60,000 bribery network**: A relatively modest sum by Cold War standards purchased extensive influence—funding newspaper editors to print false stories, ayatollahs to denounce Mosaddegh as communist, and mobilizing street demonstrations. The operation demonstrated how targeted capital could manufacture political reality. [Kongzi: 1, 3, 5; Rumi]

- **CIA-SIS joint operation**: The coup represented collaboration between American and British intelligence services, with the British Secret Intelligence Service (SIS/MI6) working alongside the CIA to reverse Mosaddegh's nationalization of the Anglo-Iranian Oil Company. [Kongzi: 1, 4]

### The Mechanics of Manufactured Consent

- **Bazaari merchant mobilization**: Tehran's merchant class, traditional pillars of Iranian society, were mobilized through economic anxiety and foreign payments rather than genuine political conviction. Their participation lent the coup an appearance of indigenous commercial opposition. [Kongzi; Rumi]

- **Rent-a-mob operations**: Demonstrators were drawn from Tehran's southern slums and athletic clubs—individuals who "did not know they served British Petroleum's interests; they knew only that shouting brought bread." This technique of employing economically desperate populations for political theater became a recurring feature of covert operations. [Rumi]

- **Clerical involvement**: Newly declassified British documents confirm "large sums of money" flowing from the U.S. Embassy to "influential people," including clerics who denounced Mosaddegh. This compromised religious establishment's moral authority by subordinating it to foreign intelligence objectives. [Kongzi: 5]

- **Tudeh party manipulation**: The genuinely communist Tudeh party was baited into premature exposure, allowing Mosaddegh's democratic nationalism to be deliberately conflated with Soviet threat through repetition alone. [Rumi]

- **Media manipulation**: Newspaper editors were paid to publish lies they knew to be false, creating an information environment in which fabricated narratives displaced observable reality. [Rumi]

### The Shah's Waffling Loyalty

- **Mohammad Reza Pahlavi's vacillation**: The Shah fled Iran, then Rome, returning only when assured of American protection—demonstrating that his authority derived not from indigenous support but from foreign power. His "waffling" revealed the puppet dynamics of the restored monarchy. [Kongzi; Rumi]

- **Fazlollah Zahedi's CIA elevation**: The general installed as Mosaddegh's replacement gained power through CIA design rather than "Heaven's mandate or people's trust," establishing a pattern of foreign-selected leadership. [Kongzi]

### The SAVAK Dictatorship and Its Consequences

- **25-year secret police regime**: The coup's immediate aftermath saw establishment of SAVAK, trained by CIA and Mossad, whose torture methods became legendary and whose files later provided Revolutionary Guards their enemies lists. [Rumi]

- **From autocracy to darker autocracy**: Iranians exchanged Mosaddegh's nationalist government for a dictatorship more brutal and more dependent on foreign support than its predecessor. [Kongzi: 7]

- **The 1979 revolution as legible response**: Khomeini's followers understood Mosaddegh's fate as "America's original sin against Iran"; the embassy takeover held hostages precisely 444 days—one day longer than Mosaddegh had governed—constituting "ritual exorcism" of 1953. [Rumi]

### The Template Effect: Operation Ajax as Prototype

- **Direct operational lineage**: Kermit Roosevelt, having succeeded in Tehran, subsequently helped plan the Bay of Pigs disaster—demonstrating how success bred hubris and transferred techniques to inappropriate contexts. [Kongzi: 6]

- **PBSUCCESS in Guatemala (1954)**: The following year's Guatemala operation explicitly drew upon Ajax methods, establishing a pattern of covert regime change against elected nationalist governments. [Kongzi: 6]

- **U.S.-Saudi intelligence partnership in Yemen**: The regional intelligence architecture forged in 1953's aftermath extended to Arabian Peninsula operations, creating enduring structures of covert cooperation. [Kongzi]

- **The simulacrum innovation**: Ajax pioneered not brute force but "simulacrum"—the simulation of popular will so convincing that participants themselves could believe in its authenticity. This technique of manufactured democratic appearance became central to subsequent operations. [Rumi]

### Economic and Geopolitical Restructuring

- **Anglo-Iranian Oil Company restoration**: The AIOC (later BP) regained its position, confirming that economic interests—specifically petroleum access—drove intervention against resource nationalism. [Kongzi: 4, 7]

- **Cold War framing**: Mosaddegh's neutralist nationalism was systematically misrepresented as communist alignment, establishing a pattern whereby independent development threatened Western economic interests would be opposed under anti-Soviet pretexts. [Kongzi: 4; Rumi]
