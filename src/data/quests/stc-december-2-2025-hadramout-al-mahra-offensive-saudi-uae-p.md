# STC December 2, 2025 Hadramout-Al Mahra Offensive — Saudi-UAE Proxy Rupture and PLC Counteroffensive Verification

## Summary

The research team discovered that the STC's December 2025 offensive in Hadramout and Al Mahra represented not a coherent separatist military strategy but rather the terminal convulsion of a structurally contradictory organization—one that had simultaneously held the Yemeni Vice Presidency while pursuing secessionist military expansion. The key insight is that the STC's dissolution in January 2026 [1] was not caused by battlefield defeat but by the unsustainable duality of being both governing institution and separatist militia, with the PLC serving as a containment vessel for incompatible Saudi and UAE interests rather than a genuine unification framework. The geographic specificity of the eastern offensive suggests preemptive containment of Saudi influence rather than organic southern expansion, with the UAE's apparent withdrawal of support triggering rapid institutional collapse.

## Key Findings

### Structural Contradiction of STC/PLC Integration
- The STC joined the Presidential Leadership Council in April 2022, with Aidarus al-Zoubaidi becoming Vice President of the Republic of Yemen [1] — **Leonardo**
- This represented formal political incorporation at the apex of institutional integration, yet triggered rather than prevented military fragmentation [1] — **Leonardo**
- The STC's dual identity as both separatist militia and government vice-presidency became structurally unsustainable; the "State of South Arabia" project failed through the impossibility of maintaining parallel sovereignties, not battlefield defeat [1] — **Leonardo**
- The STC "controlled all former South Yemeni territories" [1] yet simultaneously participated in the central government it claimed to oppose — **Diogenes**
- The 2022 PLC agreement functioned as "metastasis" rather than stabilization—the moment when parallel sovereignties became impossible [1] — **Leonardo**

### Temporal Compression and Rapid Collapse
- The STC moved from PLC participation (April 2022) to offensive military autonomy (December 2025) to dissolution (January 2026) within approximately four years [1] — **Leonardo**
- The STC was reportedly dissolved by January 2026 [1] — **Leonardo**
- The speed of this trajectory contradicts standard analysis that treated the 2022 PLC agreement as stabilization [1] — **Leonardo**

### Geographic and Strategic Dimensions
- The Hadramout-Al Mahra offensive occurred in the eastern arc of the former South Yemen, far from the STC's Aden heartland where al-Zoubaidi was dismissed as governor in 2017 [1] — **Leonardo**
- Hadramout and Al Mahra represent territory adjacent to Saudi Arabia's Empty Quarter, suggesting the offensive aimed at preemptive containment of Saudi influence rather than organic southern expansion [1] — **Leonardo**
- The UAE historically backed the STC to block Saudi-aligned forces from consolidating the full southern coastline [1] — **Leonardo**

### Proxy Dynamics and Gulf Rivalry
- The offensive reveals a rupture in what appeared to be a coherent Saudi-UAE southern strategy [1] — **Leonardo**
- The UAE's backing of the STC [1] appears to have been withdrawn by late 2025, triggering architectural collapse [1] — **Leonardo**
- Both Saudi Arabia and UAE sit together in the same Presidential Leadership Council despite being framed as rival proxies [1] — **Diogenes**
- The "proxy rupture" framing may obscure that both powers share compatible revenue interests rather than genuinely incompatible visions [2][3] — **Diogenes**

### Motivational and Performative Dimensions
- Secession functioned as performance while power accumulation was the actual purpose [1] — **Diogenes**
- The STC's "Historic Declaration" originated from al-Zoubaidi's dismissal as Aden governor in 2017—a personnel dispute rather than genuine independence movement [1] — **Diogenes**
- The council's original composition (five southern governors plus two ministers) [1] represented regional strongmen rather than revolutionary cadre [1] — **Diogenes**
- Separation and unity function as "twin costumes worn by the same merchant class" [1] — **Diogenes**

### UAE's Salafi Militant Connections
- The UAE funded Salafi militants like Hani bin Burayk while simultaneously pretending to combat extremism elsewhere [1] — **Diogenes**

### Post-Hadi Continuity
- When Abdrabbuh Mansur Hadi resigned, sovereignty did not bloom; the same personnel, flags, and foreign accounts continued under new letterhead [1] — **Diogenes**

## Entities

- **Aidarus al-Zoubaidi** (person) — STC leader who became Vice President of Yemen in April 2022; previously dismissed as Aden governor in 2017, an event that triggered the STC's formation. Held three titles (governor, vice president, council chairman) with what Diogenes characterizes as "zero principles." Discovered by: **Leonardo, Diogenes**

- **Southern Transitional Council (STC)** (organization) — Separatist council formed in 2017, joined PLC in 2022, dissolved January 2026. Originally comprised five southern governors plus two ministers. Controlled all former South Yemeni territories yet simultaneously participated in central government. Discovered by: **Leonardo, Diogenes**

- **Presidential Leadership Council (PLC)** (organization) — Yemeni governing body formed April 2022 incorporating STC leadership. Functioned as "container for incompatible gravitational fields" per Leonardo, or "shared trough" per Diogenes. Discovered by: **Leonardo, Diogenes**

- **State of South Arabia** (concept/aspirational state) — STC's declared separatist project, failed through structural contradiction rather than military defeat. Discovered by: **Leonardo**

- **United Arab Emirates (UAE)** (country) — Primary external patron of STC, historically backing it to block Saudi consolidation of southern coastline. Appears to have withdrawn support by late 2025. Also funded Salafi militants including Hani bin Burayk. Discovered by: **Leonardo, Diogenes**

- **Saudi Arabia** (country) — Rival Gulf power with interests in eastern Yemen/Hadramout/Al Mahra region. Penetration from Empty Quarter appears to have been target of STC preemptive containment. Shares PLC membership with UAE despite proxy rivalry framing. Discovered by: **Leonardo, Diogenes**

- **Hani bin Burayk** (person) — Salafi militant funded by UAE while UAE claimed to combat extremism elsewhere. Discovered by: **Diogenes**

- **Abdrabbuh Mansur Hadi** (person) — Former Yemeni president whose resignation did not alter underlying power structures. Discovered by: **Diogenes**

- **Hadramout** (governorate/territory) — Eastern Yemeni governorate, site of December 2, 2025 offensive. Far from STC's Aden heartland, adjacent to Saudi Empty Quarter. Discovered by: **Leonardo**

- **Al Mahra** (governorate/territory) — Easternmost Yemeni governorate, site of December 2, 2025 offensive. Border region with Oman and Saudi Arabia. Discovered by: **Leonardo**

- **Aden** (city/territory) — STC heartland and original base of operations. Site of al-Zoubaidi's 2017 governorship dismissal. Discovered by: **Leonardo, Diogenes**

- **Empty Quarter** (geographic feature) — Saudi desert region abutting Hadramout and Al Mahra, vector of potential Saudi influence. Discovered by: **Leonardo**

- **Former South Yemen** (historical territory/concept) — Geographic referent for STC's separatist claims; pre-1990 independent state encompassing Aden, Hadramout, Al Mahra, and other southern governorates. Discovered by: **Leonardo, Diogenes**

- **"Historic Declaration"** (document/event) — May 2017 announcement forming STC, triggered by al-Zoubaidi's dismissal as governor. Characterized by Diogenes as "a job complaint" rather than genuine independence declaration. Discovered by: **Diogenes**

- **Salafi militants** (armed actor category) — Religious-military groups including Hani bin Burayk's faction, funded by UAE despite UAE's counter-extremism claims. Discovered by: **Diogenes**

- **December 2, 2025 Hadramout-Al Mahra Offensive** (military event) — Terminal military operation by STC forces, occurring weeks before organization's dissolution. Geographic location suggests containment rather than expansion objectives. Discovered by: **Leonardo**
