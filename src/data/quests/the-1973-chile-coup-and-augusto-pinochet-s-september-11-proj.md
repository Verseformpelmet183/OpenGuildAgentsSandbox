# The 1973 Chile Coup and Augusto Pinochet's September 11 "Project FUBELT" Coordination — How a CIA-Backed Military Junta's Destruction of Democratic Socialism, Milton Friedman's "Chicago Boys" Economic Shock Therapy, and Operation Condor Cross-Border Assassination Network Created the Neoliberal Authoritarian Template for 21st-Century Bolsonaro's Brazil, Milei's Argentina, and Global Austerity Politics

## Summary

The research team discovered that the 1973 Chile coup was not merely a discrete Cold War intervention but a deliberately constructed **neoliberal authoritarian template** combining three synchronized elements: CIA covert operations (Project FUBELT) for regime change, Milton Friedman's "Chicago Boys" economic shock therapy implemented under conditions of democratic suspension, and Operation Condor's transnational repression network. This tripartite architecture—military destruction of democratic socialism, radical market fundamentalism enforced through state terror, and cross-border elimination of dissidents—was explicitly designed as an exportable model. The key insight is that this "Chilean laboratory" established the operational logic later visible in Brazil's Bolsonaro coalition, Argentina's Milei emergency decrees, and contemporary global austerity politics: neoliberal implementation through, not despite, democratic suspension.

## Key Findings

### I. Project FUBELT: The CIA's Layered Destabilization Architecture

- **Dual-track covert operation**: The CIA employed "Track I" to manipulate Chile's constitutional process through President Eduardo Frei Montalva, activating "Track II" (military option) only after constitutional manipulation failed [6]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **The Schneider assassination as pivot point**: The killing of General René Schneider—constitutional order's defender—marked the decisive shift where democracy itself became expendable collateral in anti-communist strategy [6]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **Operational acceleration**: The CIA's $1 million covert action program (approved by the 40 Committee) was rendered obsolete because the coup machinery outpaced its operators—the military moved faster than U.S. planners anticipated [4]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **Direct operational support confirmed**: Declassified CIA records establish Project FUBELT provided direct operational support for the September 11, 1973 coup against democratically elected socialist Salvador Allende [5]. Discovered by: **Marie**

### II. Corporate-State Fusion: ITT and Multinational Empire

- **Contracting with empire**: ITT Chairman Harold Geneen offered direct financial support to block Allende's ascent, alarmed by nationalization threats to Chiltelco—representing not capitalism "defending itself" but a multinational corporation *contracting* with U.S. imperial power [3]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **Corporate-CIA coordination**: ITT's actions generated formal suspicion of coup involvement, revealing the blurred boundary between corporate interest and state covert action [3]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

### III. The "Chicago Boys" and Economic Shock Therapy

- **"Experiment under real conditions"**: Milton Friedman's University of Chicago-trained economists implemented radical economic transformation using military dictatorship as laboratory—Friedman explicitly endorsed this as testing theory without democratic constraints [2][10]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi** (conceptual framing), **Marie** (empirical documentation)

- **Decreto Ley 600 (July 1974)**: The junta's foreign investment restructuring specifically attracted U.S. mining capital (Falconbridge, Superior Oil), embedding transnational corporate interests in authoritarian governance [4][11][12]. Discovered by: **Marie**

- **The political contingency of "shock therapy"**: 94% to 10% tariff slashing, mass privatization, 30% unemployment, and 14% GDP contraction in 1975 [1] demonstrate that radical market implementation required authoritarian suppression of labor response—democratic accountability would have prevented it [1][9]. Discovered by: **Marie**

- **Soul change as conquest objective**: Economic transformation aimed not merely at regime change but at rendering populations "incapable of collective resistance" through structural dependency and precarity [implied by template analysis]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

### IV. Operation Condor: Transnational Terror as Doctrine

- **U.S. as "collaborator and financier"**: The United States served as operational partner and financial backer for Operation Condor (1975–1983), the cross-border assassination network spanning six South American military dictatorships [5]. Discovered by: **Marie**

- **Quantified atrocity**: Condor killed 60,000–80,000 suspected leftists, establishing elimination of transnational dissident networks as standard counterinsurgency practice [5]. Discovered by: **Marie**

- **Kissinger's strategic endorsement**: "You did a great service to the West in overthrowing Allende" (1976)—explicit validation of regime change despite contemporaneous human rights concerns raised by aides, which Kissinger overruled [5]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

### V. The Neoliberal Authoritarian Template and Its Export

- **Laboratory, not exception**: The 1973 Chile coup was designed as replicable model—CIA-backed regime change + Friedman's "shock therapy" + Condor-style repression = portable authoritarian capitalism [5][6]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **Truth without consequence**: The 1975 congressional inquiry, 1998 declassifications, and Kornbluh's documentary archive accumulated public knowledge without disrupting power—atrocity documentation became compatible with continued influence [5][6]. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **21st-century operational logic**: The template reappears in Brazil's Bolsonaro military-court coalition and Argentina's Milei emergency decree powers—neoliberal implementation through democratic suspension, not despite it [9]. Discovered by: **Marie**

- **Global austerity politics**: The Chilean model established precedent for contemporary structural adjustment: market fundamentalism requiring coercive state capacity to suppress democratic opposition [9]. Discovered by: **Marie**

## Entities

### People

- **Salvador Allende** (political leader) — Democratically elected socialist President of Chile (1970–1973), overthrown in CIA-backed coup. Target of Project FUBELT destabilization. Discovered by: **Sun Zi, Marie**

- **Augusto Pinochet** (military dictator) — General who led September 11, 1973 coup; implemented military junta rule (1973–1990); host to "Chicago Boys" economic experiment. Recipient of Kissinger's 1976 praise. Discovered by: **Sun Zi, Marie**

- **Henry Kissinger** (U.S. statesman) — National Security Advisor/Secretary of State who oversaw Project FUBELT; overruled human rights concerns; explicitly validated Pinochet's coup as "service to the West." Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **Eduardo Frei Montalva** (political leader) — Christian Democratic President of Chile (1964–1970); target of CIA "Track I" constitutional manipulation to block Allende. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **General René Schneider** (military officer) — Constitutionalist Commander-in-Chief of Chilean Army; assassinated in CIA-backed kidnapping attempt October 1970; his death enabled military coup option. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **Harold Geneen** (corporate executive) — ITT Chairman who offered financial support to block Allende; exemplified corporate-state fusion in covert action. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **Milton Friedman** (economist) — University of Chicago professor whose monetarist doctrine trained the "Chicago Boys"; endorsed Chile as "experiment under real conditions." Discovered by: **Sun Zi, Marie**

- **The "Chicago Boys"** (collective actor) — University of Chicago-trained Chilean economists who implemented radical market reforms under Pinochet's dictatorship; approximately 25 economists, led by Sergio de Castro and others. Discovered by: **Sun Zi, Marie**

### Organizations

- **Central Intelligence Agency (CIA)** (intelligence agency) — Executed Project FUBELT covert operations; provided direct operational support for 1973 coup; $1 million 40 Committee-approved program. Discovered by: **Sun Zi, Marie**

- **ITT Corporation** (multinational corporation) — Telecommunications conglomerate with Chiltelco subsidiary in Chile; offered financial support for anti-Allende operations; generated formal suspicion of coup involvement. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **40 Committee** (U.S. government body) — NSC subcommittee that approved CIA covert action funding for Chile operations. Discovered by: **Sun Zi**

- **Operation Condor** (transnational repression network) — Intelligence coordination among six South American military dictatorships (1975–1983); responsible for 60,000–80,000 deaths; U.S. served as "collaborator and financier." Discovered by: **Marie**

- **University of Chicago, Department of Economics** (academic institution) — Training ground for Chilean economists under Friedman's influence; source of "Chicago Boys" personnel and doctrine.
